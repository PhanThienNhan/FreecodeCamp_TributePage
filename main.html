1. Pham Quoc Viet - Team leader of the FAS Angel first aid support team.

  • Pham Quoc Viet, born in 1987, hails from Nam Dinh, and currently resides and works in Hanoi. He is the founder of FAS Angel (First Aid Support Angel), an organization dedicated to providing first aid assistance.

  • The establishment of the team stemmed from Pham Quoc Viet's feelings of loneliness and abandonment after he was involved in a traffic accident in Tuyen Quang in 2016. Subsequently, Viet was determined to apply the first aid knowledge he had learned during his military service to begin a journey of assisting those in need on the streets of the capital city.

  • Up to now, on average, the FAS Angel team has carried out over 1,000 first aid cases per year, including approximately 600 traffic accidents of varying severity.

  • The contributions of the FAS Angel free first aid support team have been recognized and highly appreciated. The team has been awarded certificates of merit at the National Volunteer Awards and other badges of honor.

  • In the 4th "Initiative for the Community" competition in 2022, Pham Quoc Viet's project "Developing a free first aid support team for the community" was honored to receive the B Prize.

  • Most recently, FAS Angel was honored as an Inspirational Project at the Human Act Prize 2023, the Community Action Award.

The 'orange-shirt heroes' Pham Quoc Viet: "Even if I'm gone, there are still 12 siblings left. FAS Angel always lives to save people! FAS Angel is like my own child. I will nurture, care for, maintain, and develop it, especially dedicating FAS Angel to future generations. I will definitely do this because there are more and more passionate volunteers every day, ready to share the joys and sorrows with me, no longer like in 2017 - when I had to go out alone to rescue people."

2. Nguyen Thi Ha, teacher at Phan Dinh Giot High School, Dien Bien Phu City, Dien Bien Province.

  • Ms. Nguyen Thi Ha graduated with a Bachelor's degree in English pedagogy from the University of Languages and International Studies, Hanoi National University in 2004. In the same year, she began working at Phan Dinh Giot High School.

  • In 2016, Ms. Ha became the Deputy Secretary of the school's youth union alongside her professional duties. This marked the beginning of her involvement in volunteer work, providing support and assistance to students in difficult circumstances.

  • In order to support and assist a greater number of students in difficult circumstances, Ms. Ha and the teachers at Phan Dinh Giot High School established a "zero-dong supermarket" booth. Here, compassionate individuals, neighbors, colleagues, etc., have contributed whatever they can, from old/new clothes to household items, to offer aid.

  • In addition to students in remote areas, Ms. Ha has also provided assistance to many cases near the city. One such case is Tran Dinh An Na, a student in class 6A1 at Thanh Luong Secondary School, who comes from extremely difficult circumstances. Her mother suffers from scleroderma (a rare and incurable disease worldwide), leaving her unable to work. Na's younger sister has lymphoma, and the family lacks the financial means to afford her treatment. During a visit to Na's family, her mother recognized Ms. Ha as her former teacher. The two embraced each other in tears, never expecting to reunite under such circumstances. True to Ms. Ha's promise, Na's family regularly receives gifts from the connections she has made with kind-hearted individuals.

"In my journey, every road, every destination I reach, I encounter many different difficult circumstances that make me unable to stop here. Although my feet may sometimes grow weary, my willpower does not allow me to stop." - Teacher Nguyen Thi Ha expressed openly.

3. Tra Thi Thu, teacher at Tra Tap ethnic boarding primary school, Tra Tap commune, Nam Tra My district, Quang Nam Province.

  • After completing a two-year primary education pedagogy program, fueled by her love for children and her independence from a young age, Tra Thi Thu persuaded her family and wrote a letter to the Nam Tra My district, Quang Nam province, to turn her childhood dream into reality.

  • In that small school, with its clear-eyed children and meager command of the Vietnamese language, Tra Thi Thu faced 40 students with worn faces. Initially, during the day, she spoke her part, while the students spoke theirs. At night, the young teacher wiped away tears of fear, loneliness, and homesickness that never seemed to fade. The unimaginable difficulties made Thu briefly consider leaving her beloved job. However, day after day, the hesitant reading voices of the kind-hearted children from the highlands, the purity they exuded, and the warmth of the villagers' support, all kept the young teacher grounded.

  • When introduced to volunteer teachers at the school, Tra Thi Thu joined the Love Connection Club in Nam Tra My district. From there, her newfound passion for volunteer trips on holidays helped her learn how to directly mobilize support and aid for students and local residents at the teaching site.

  • For nine months, Tra Thi Thu provided loving care for students, ensuring they received nutritious meals. She also organized over 300 aid packages for local residents, totaling over 120 million VND. These achievements were made possible through her efforts. Additionally, she personally volunteered to help disadvantaged residents in her district for over 20 days, directly cooking meals and providing milk for the students.

  • During the 2017-2018 academic year, Ms. Tra Thi Thu taught at the Mo Roi school site. She continued her efforts by mobilizing 540 daily meals for 10 students who stayed at school, providing 72 nutritious meals for all students, distributing 50 crates of milk, and offering over 300 aid packages to local residents, totaling 174 million VND.

    In addition, Ms. Thu went above and beyond by providing all necessary conditions and personally caring for 10 young children, aged 3 to 6, from distant homes, allowing them to stay and study at the school throughout the week. Balancing her role as a teacher and a nurturing mother figure, she took care of these children, ensuring they had not only meals and rest but also comforting them when they fell ill or felt homesick.

"The joy of my work intertwines with a growing passion every day, to the point where I feel like I am a part of this majestic highland region," expressed Teacher Tra Thi Thu, who has been dedicated to the mountainous district of Nam Tra My, Quang Nam, for over 5 years.

5. Nguyen Truong Giang, head of the Green Cricket Club, Ca Mau Province.

  • After participating in environmental protection campaigns, Nguyen Truong Giang (born in 2005), a student at the Vietnam - Korea Vocational College (Cà Mau), mobilized classmates to form a group to clean up parks, bus stations, school gates, temples, and pagodas. They shared images of their activities on the social media platform Facebook. Initially, the group consisted of about a dozen people, but gradually more young people in the city joined. To formalize their activities, at the end of 2022, Giang founded the Green Grasshopper Club.

  • Currently, the club has 72 members and over 100 volunteers from both within and outside the province, mainly students aged 15 to 20. Giang has built and organized multiple teams, including a "grasshopper" team to survey and hunt for litter black spots, as well as establishing a hotline to receive information from the public with the motto "if there's trash, let's handle it."

  • In April 2023, the club became a member of the Vietnam Youth Union in Cà Mau City. This facilitated more favorable conditions for the club to connect and implement activities, eradicating more litter black spots. Moreover, from this point, the club's activities spread further, attracting more young people and residents to participate.

  • To raise funds, the Green Grasshopper Club organized recycling activities to turn plastic waste into handmade products. Each week, the club produces 15 to 20 items such as trash cans, pen holders, flower pots, etc., from recycled materials. Through the channels of the Youth Union and other organizations, the recycled products made by these "green grasshoppers" are in high demand.

  • Since its establishment, the Green Grasshopper Club has organized over 70 independent activities and participated in coordinating 45 additional activities. Through these efforts, the club has collected and processed over 40 tons of waste, including more than 15 tons of plastic waste.

"We believe that small, meaningful actions, within the capacity of our age group, can attract many young people to participate. We hope to bring about significant changes in the self-awareness and actions of adults and the community to protect the environment and promote a healthy living environment." - "Grasshopper Uncle" Nguyen Truong Giang, Chairman of the Green Grasshopper Club.
